What we do

HIV/AIDS MITIGATION

We take pride in supporting responsive HIV/AIDs Awareness, Prevention and Training services in the communities where we work in.  AMREC believes in localized solutions and interventions that actively promote HIV awareness among the communities where we operate. We do so through evidence-based interventions that have been tested and found to be effective in other rural and peri-urban settings. We are experienced in Social Behavior Change Communication (SBCC) and undertaken various assignment including consultancies in HIV/AIDs Awareness, Prevention and Training services in infrastructure projects.
Our HIV/AIDS mitigation campaign is aimed at stopping HIV stigma and promoting HIV testing, prevention, and treatment. The campaign is designed to reach a wide range of people, the teenagers, youths and young adults, women and men. We also undertake Workplace HIV/AIDS Awareness, Prevention and Training services.

We partner with The National Syndemic Diseases Control Council (NSDCC), and the National AIDS and STI's Control Programme (NASCOP) to support the initiative, dubbed 'Jiinue Sasa which aims to bring to an end the triple threat of HIV infection, Social Gender Based Violence (SGBV) and Adolescent pregnancies through community dialogue that encourages parents to provide sex education and reproductive health to their children. We have a strategic focus on the following thematic interventions; HIV Stigma, HIV Testing, HIV Prevention and HIV Treatment
 

Climate change

Climate change may affect our health and wellbeing through the impacts of extreme events, worsening air quality, changes in the spread of infectious diseases, threats to food and water quality and quantity and effects on our mental health.
There are things we can all do now to build our resilience to the effects of climate change and help slow its pace.
The consequences of climate change now include, among others, intense droughts, water scarcity, severe fires, rising sea levels, flooding, melting polar ice, catastrophic storms and declining biodiversity.
AMREC has programmes to address climate in its areas of operation by establishing and partnering with environment clubs in schools and churches to plant and maintain trees in schools and homes. AMREC supports environmental champions with non school items such as bags, pens and pencils and exercise books
We partner with various stakeholders including the Kenya Forest Services to plant trees in schools where we operate.
Spending much time around trees enhances students' academic performance, mental and physical well-being, concentration, and self-discipline as well as their love of nature
 

Research services

We conduct high-quality research covering community-based surveys, cohort studies, and randomized trials. We boast of a large pool of expertise in various fields ranging from statisticians, epidemiologists, public and community health experts, ICT and computing, mathematical modeling etc.  AMREC conducts project and program evaluations through baseline, midline and end-line surveys, impact assessments, Community benefit assessment and planning for nonprofit organizations. We support organizations and individuals to evaluate the effectiveness of their projects and value for money.  The AMREC team has experience in analyzing programs and services and evaluating results. We assist with External evaluation for grant-funded projects, Formative and summative evaluation [Needs Assessments, Baseline surveys, KAP Surveys, Mid-term, and End term evaluations], Data collection and analysis, Product evaluation for business growth, Evaluation of conferences, events or training programs, Customer and employee satisfaction surveys.  We also support development of M&E Systems including data collection tools.
Our staff are experienced in entering large amounts of data, conducting data analysis, and participating in ongoing research and data collection for any research projects. 
We use a variety of statistical packages e.g., SPSS, STATA, Epi Info, Epi Data, R, SAS and ensure we have the best that suits your dataset.  Our team of statisticians have experience conducting multiple data analyses including, but not limited to: 

  • Descriptive Analysis

Water, Hygiene and Sanitation

Goal No. 6 of the sustainable development goals is Ensure access to water and sanitation for all. Water, sanitation, and hygiene (WASH) are crucial building blocks for development—WASH interventions improve health outcomes, advance education, and reduce poverty and malnutrition.
Between 2015 and 2020, the population with safely managed sanitation increased from 47 per cent to 54 per cent and the population with access to handwashing facilities with soap and water in the home increased from 67 per cent to 71 per cent. Rates of progress for these basic services would need to quadruple for universal coverage to be reached by 2030.
Poor sanitation, which is widely accepted as a chief contributor to waterborne diseases, causes the deaths of more than 1,200 children under age 5 every day—-more than from AIDS, measles, and tuberculosis combined. AMREC works with local communities to build capacities and contribute to improved sanitation outcomes through trainings and awareness creation. 
 

Drug and substance Abuse sensitization

According to the World Health Organization (WHO), in 2016, 3.3 million people died out of alcohol and drug abuse, and 12.2 % of Kenyan population aged between the age of 15-65 years are alcohol consumers.
To contribute to mitigation of the adverse effects of drugs and substance abuse, AMREC works with school administrators to conduct regular sensitization sessions among secondary schools in counties where we operate. We do this through various platforms including theatre.
We also seek to establish and support peer clubs in schools and training institutions to help empower others. We hold discussions on the relationship between drug and substance abuse and gender-based violence and the spread of HIV/AIDS.
 

Mental health Awareness

Mental illnesses are health challenges that can affect the way we think about ourselves, relate to others, and interact with the world around us. They affect our thoughts, feelings, and behaviours. Mental illnesses can disrupt a person’s life or create challenges, but with the right supports, a person can get back on a path to sustainability and wellness. A staggering 1 out of 4 persons who seek healthcare in Kenya have a mental health condition.
AMREC works with experienced mental health experts to provide sensitization among communities. We support professional counsellors to provide counselling services to those affected while encouraging stigma reduction.
